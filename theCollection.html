Presentation of the collection

The COR-IPHES osteological reference collection is a unique scientific resource curated by the Institut Català de Paleoecologia Humana i Evolució Social (IPHES). It comprises a wide range of mammal and bird skeletons, meticulously prepared to support research in archaeology, paleontology, and related fields.

The COR-IPHES collection includes over 200 individual skeletons, representing species from Europe and Africa, ranging from common mammals to rare or endangered species like the European bison and the Iberian lynx. These specimens are essential for comparative studies, enabling researchers to analyze and identify animal remains from archaeological and paleontological sites. The collection is particularly valuable in zooarchaeology, where precise skeletal comparisons help reconstruct ancient environments and human-animal interactions.

This collection is actively expanding and serves not only as a research tool but also as a cornerstone for educational programs. Each specimen is carefully documented with detailed metadata, and efforts are underway to digitize the entire collection for broader accessibility. Through 3D scanning and open access, COR-IPHES aims to support global scientific collaboration and foster new discoveries in the study of past ecosystems and species.
