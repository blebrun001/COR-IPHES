Presentation of the project

The Esqueletos en Línea project is developed within the framework of IPHES-CERCA’s commitment to advancing open science and enhancing access to scientific collections. The project is supported by the María de Guzmán 2023 program, funded by the Fundación Española para la Ciencia y la Tecnología (FECYT), which aims to promote excellence in scientific research and the dissemination of open access data.

The Esqueletos en Línea project aims to digitize and provide online access to the osteological reference collection of the Institut Català de Paleoecologia Humana i Evolució Social (IPHES). This collection, which includes specimens from various mammals and birds, serves as a crucial resource for researchers in archaeology, paleontology, and biology. Through this project, the specimens are being digitized in 3D and made available via an open-access digital repository, enabling remote access and facilitating scientific comparison.

The project is designed to promote open science by providing researchers and the public with standardized, easily reusable data, in alignment with the FAIR principles (Findable, Accessible, Interoperable, Reusable). Each specimen is accompanied by detailed metadata following the Darwin Core standard, ensuring interoperability with other international scientific databases, such as GBIF. This approach enhances the visibility and utilization of the collection on a global scale, while adhering to the highest standards in data management.

In addition to online access to the specimens, the project includes a robust digital infrastructure that supports 3D visualization, the export of models for 3D printing, and integration into collaborative research initiatives. By facilitating access to this unique collection, IPHES-CERCA supports research and educational initiatives, while contributing to the preservation and protection of its scientific heritage.

CORA-RDR

The 3D models data of the COR-IPHES osteological collection are hosted within the IPHES Dataverse, a digital repository that facilitates the storage, management, and dissemination of research data. This infrastructure is supported by CORA-RDR, a consortium-based initiative aimed at providing robust, sustainable repositories for academic institutions in Catalonia. The technical solution behind this system, Dataverse, is an open-source software developed to manage research datasets with a focus on data sharing, long-term preservation, and open science principles. Dataverse enables users to deposit, explore, and reuse datasets, while ensuring metadata compliance and supporting interoperability through standardized formats such as Darwin Core. By leveraging this infrastructure, the COR-IPHES collection is made widely accessible to the global scientific community, promoting collaboration and research across disciplines.
